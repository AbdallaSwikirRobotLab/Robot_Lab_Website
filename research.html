Research Overview

AIRoCS advances intelligent robotics and control with emphasis on safe learning, aerial manipulation, vision-language navigation, SLAM on embedded platforms, and multi-robot swarms. We span theory, algorithms, and deployment on real systems.

Safety Learning-Based Control Aerial Manipulation Vision-Language SLAM & Perception Swarms

Projects

Automatic Control

Automatic control designs feedback laws that make complex systems behave reliably under uncertainty. We study compositional analysis of large-scale interconnected systems, symbolic control, and control barrier functions (CBFs) to guarantee safety and stability, and integrate these with learning for performance.

  • Barrier-function shielding for RL controllers.
  • Scalable verification for networked robotic systems.
  • Model predictive control with learned dynamics.

Robot Learning

We develop data-efficient methods that combine models and priors with modern learning, enabling robots to adapt on-the-fly while respecting constraints.

Imitation Learning

Imitation learning accelerates skill acquisition by leveraging expert demonstrations. We study dataset aggregation, risk-sensitive clones, and policy refinement with online safety filters to bridge the sim-to-real gap in manipulation and navigation.

Compute & Simulation
